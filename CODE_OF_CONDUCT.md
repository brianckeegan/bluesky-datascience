# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of **bluesky-datascience** pledge to
make participation in our community a welcoming, safe, and equitable experience
for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance,
race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This is a teaching project: many participants are students, journalists, and
researchers who are new to programming, to APIs, or to social-media research. We
pledge to act and interact in ways that lower the barrier to learning and that
contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Encouraged Behaviors

While acknowledging differences in social norms, we all strive to meet our
community's expectations for positive behavior. Examples of behavior that
contributes to a positive environment include:

- Respecting the purpose of this community — helping people learn data science
  with Bluesky and the AT Protocol — and the people who participate in it.
- Being considerate, kind, constructive, and helpful, especially toward
  newcomers asking beginner questions.
- Gracefully giving and accepting constructive feedback on code, notebooks,
  and writing.
- Taking responsibility for our mistakes, apologizing to those affected, and
  learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall
  community of learners and researchers.
- Valuing diverse perspectives, methods, and disciplinary backgrounds.
- Crediting the work and ideas of others, including data sources, prior
  research, and the people whose public posts appear in examples.

## Unacceptable Behaviors

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of
  any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information — such as a physical or email address,
  or de-anonymized account data collected via the API — without their explicit
  permission ("doxxing").
- Using project data, code, or examples to surveil, harass, profile, or target
  individuals or groups.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or pull requests.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional or academic setting.

Because this project teaches the collection and analysis of real, public social
media data, we hold one another to an especially high standard around the
**ethical and responsible use of that data**. The research-ethics guidance in
[`AGENTS.md`](AGENTS.md) and in each notebook's "Ethics and limitations" section
is part of the spirit of this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces — the GitHub
repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code review, and commit
messages) — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the
community in public spaces. Examples include using an official email address,
posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed
representative at an online or offline event.

## Reporting an Issue

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns,
please report it by contacting the project maintainer, Brian Keegan, at
**accounts@brianckeegan.com**. For conduct on a specific item, you may also use
GitHub's [report abuse](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam)
feature.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community
leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any
incident. We will not name harassment victims without their affirmative consent.

When making a report, please include — to the extent you are comfortable —
what happened, where and when, who was involved, and any links or screenshots.
You do not need to have all of this information to make a report.

## Addressing and Repairing Harm

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining
the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct.
The goal is to repair harm and restore trust where possible, while keeping the
community safe.

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior
was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. Violating
these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period of time. Violating these
terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the
community.
